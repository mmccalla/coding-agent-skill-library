# Enhanced Agile Epic Template

**Epic Title**: [Short, descriptive title summarizing the epic]

**Epic Description**:  
As a [specific user or role],  
I want [high-level functionality or goal],  
so that [strategic benefit or outcome].

**Persona/Context**:  
[Description of the primary user(s) or stakeholder(s), their context, environment, or needs, e.g., "Alex, a frequent donor seeking a seamless giving experience on mobile and desktop."]

**Business Objective**:  
[High-level business goal or value, e.g., "Increase donor retention by 20% through an improved user experience."]

**Scope**:  
[Key features or deliverables included in the epic, e.g., "Search functionality, donation processing, and user account management."]

**Out of Scope**:  
[What is explicitly excluded to avoid scope creep, e.g., "Integration with third-party fundraising platforms."]

**Success Metrics**:  
[Measurable outcomes to evaluate the epic’s impact, e.g., "15% increase in donation conversion rate within 3 months."]

**Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)**:  
[Performance, security, or scalability requirements, e.g., "System must handle 10,000 concurrent users with <2s response time."]

**Dependencies**:  
[Dependencies on other teams, systems, or epics, e.g., "Requires payment gateway API from the backend team."]

**Visual Reference**:  
[Links to designs, prototypes, or story maps, e.g., "Figma prototype: [link to design]."]

**Priority/Business Value**:  
[Quantified value or prioritization rationale, e.g., "High priority; supports Q4 fundraising campaign with estimated $100K revenue increase."]

**Related User Stories**:  
[List of user stories or tasks under this epic, e.g., "Story #CHAR-123: Search for charities by cause or location."]

**Stakeholders**:  
[Key stakeholders or teams involved, e.g., "Product Owner: Jane Doe, Backend Team, UX Team."]

**Timeline/Target Release**:  
[Estimated timeline or target release, e.g., "Q4 2025, spanning 3 sprints."]

### Example Filled Epic Template

**Epic Title**: Streamlined Donation Experience for Individual Donors

**Epic Description**:  
As an individual donor,  
I want a seamless and intuitive donation process on the giving website,  
so that I can easily find and support charities that align with my values.

**Persona/Context**:  
Sarah, a 28-year-old teacher, frequently donates small amounts to education and community-focused charities via mobile or desktop. She values simplicity, trust, and quick interactions, often donating while browsing social media.

**Business Objective**:  
Increase donor engagement and conversion rates by 20% through a user-friendly donation platform, supporting the charity’s Q4 fundraising campaign.

**Scope**:  
- Charity search by cause or location.  
- One-click donation process with multiple payment options.  
- Donation confirmation and receipt generation.  
- Basic user account features (e.g., saved payment methods, donation history).

**Out of Scope**:  
- Advanced user account features (e.g., recurring donations, donor profiles).  
- Integration with third-party fundraising platforms.

**Success Metrics**:  
- 20% increase in donation conversion rate within 3 months.  
- 85% of users complete a donation within 2 minutes of landing on the site.  
- 90% user satisfaction score in post-donation surveys.

**Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)**:  
- System must handle 10,000 concurrent users with response times under 2 seconds.  
- Must comply with PCI DSS for secure payment processing.  
- Website must meet WCAG 2.1 accessibility standards.

**Dependencies**:  
- Backend team to provide search and payment APIs.  
- UX team to deliver mobile-responsive designs.  
- Dependency on Epic #PAY-001: Payment Gateway Integration.

**Visual Reference**:  
- Figma prototype: [link to donation platform designs].  
- Story map attached in Jira epic #CHAR-999.

**Priority/Business Value**:  
- High priority; expected to drive $100K in additional donations for Q4 2025 campaign.

**Related User Stories**:  
- Story #CHAR-456: Search for charities by cause or location.  
- Story #CHAR-123: One-click donation for individual donors.  
- Story #CHAR-457: Generate donation confirmation email.

**Stakeholders**:  
- Product Owner: Jane Doe.  
- Teams: Backend, UX/UI, QA, Marketing.  
- External: Payment processor (Stripe).

**Timeline/Target Release**:  
- Q4 2025, spanning 3 sprints (6 weeks).
